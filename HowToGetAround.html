How to get around

  1. Public transportation
    • Toronto has a system of buses, and subways that link the entire city called the TTC (Toronto Transit Commission).
    • Cash fares are 3.25 CAD or 3.20 CAD if you have a reloadable PRESTO card (the card costs 6 CAD).
    • Also, you can purchase a day pass for 13.50 CAD!
  2. Car rental
    • Car rentals can be found for as little as 30 CAD per day.
    • There are many car rental options out there for all budgets!
    • A good option is to use a price comparison aggregator to find the best car for your trip: RentalCars or AutoEurope.
  3. Bike rental
    • Bike Share Toronto offers daily passes for 7 CAD and 72-hour passes for 15 CAD.
    • They have over 6,000 bikes spread between 625 stations around the city.
  4. Taxi
    • Taxi tariffs in Canada are officially set by cities or districts. The taxi tariff Toronto was last set in December 2013 and published in the official tariff ordinance.
    • The Toronto taxi tariff consists of a basic charge, various kilometer prices and a time-dependent component for standing and waiting times.
    • The basic fee is $4.25, the kilometer price is $1.75. For standing and waiting time, $31.00 is charged per hour.
